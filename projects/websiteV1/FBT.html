Only do PA template when it's a Caremark Account and PA information is found. Caremark Account means that you can find it in PeopleSafe
PA information can be located in PeopleSafe or CAS.

Always check payor file to make sure the plan doesn't require special PA handling. Some plans may only accepts PA initialization via phone call.
Don't forget to register approved PA information in SPARCS!

When filing NON-SGM PA via CoverMyMeds, make sure to choose the correct PA form. You can search the PA form with BIN, PCN, and GROUP number instead of plan name. After completing the form, always send the form to prescriber by fax, not to the plan.

If patient wants to expedite the PA process, have patient call the insurance. Do not provide number in HBS SC screen, as that's pharmacy help desk number. Insurance phone number for patient to call is located on their insurance card.

File PA for rejections: NON-FORMULARY, NDC NOT COVERED, PA REQUIRED, and PLAN LIMITATION EXCEEDED.

There are two types of PA and a medication can require more than one PA:
1) PA for 'coverage' reason. (NON-FORMULARY, NDC NOT COLOVERD, PA REQUIRED)
2) PA for 'Plan Limitation override' reason. (PLAN LIMITATION EXCEEDED)
⇊ ⇊ ⇊
Example) A medication already has approved PA on file but claim rejects for PLAN LIMITATION EXCEEDED. So another PA has been filed and couple days later, PA is approved. - Now this drug has 2 approved PA on file.

You can also have doctor to send new prescription that falls under insurance limitation to circumvent PLAN LIMITATION EXCEEDED rejection. Make sure to call insurance to verify appropriate amount before calling the doctor's office.

Always call the insurance's pharmacy help desk (shown in SC screen in HBS) to verify appropriate specialty pharmacy to triage to.
Never presume specialty pharmacy based on insurance as it can be different by the specific plan. (e.g. Many OPTUMRX plans' in-network specialty pharmacy is BRIOVARX. But you will still need to call and verify before triaging.)

External triage means "send prescription over to the in-network specialty pharmacy per patient's insurance". Plans can have several in-network specialty pharmacies.
x
Make sure you are filling out the templates completely with as much information as you can.
If the person before you already did certain templates for the same prescription, you don't have to do them unless the information changed.
You can treat Silverlink call and inbound call from patient as a Welcome call. Again, it has to be the same drug that you are working on.

If you are cancelling as CANCEL ENROLLMENT-PT AND MD AWARE, call patient and doctor to let them know of current status.
